Looking Back On The Past Three Years

Created2023-08-13|Updated2023-08-13
|Post View:

"God blesses proud people with a child's heart; here, it's not about innocence and purity, but about always being concerned only with their own likes and dislikes."

August 11, 2023, 9:28 PM California time, I'm writing this article in my apartment in Los Angeles. This piece will be more of a casual nature, without much pre-planning; I'll basically write whatever comes to mind.

Firstly, I want to say that I feel like I'm on the relatively correct path in my life, at least I don't have too much confusion and fear about the future. When I think back to two years ago during the 2021 application season, looking around, I found that everyone had a strong passion for research, but compared to that, I had a strong preference for engineering. So, I was basically unable to find my own path. Last year, I participated in the Shenzhen Science and Technology Innovation Institute, got into a state of entrepreneurship, but found myself hesitant and not doing what I loved. This year, during the first half, I joined a startup project and led an initial project, feeling that I've gained new experiences and realizing more of my shortcomings. But overall, I'm continuing down the path of engineering, looking for an engineering job, something I'm very excited about. So, I still value the opportunity to find a job in the second half of the year, and I want to use this opportunity to learn and improve.

But overall, I think I've stumbled into quite a few pitfalls over these three years and gained a lot of lessons.

I think the first major pitfall is "impatience". When I was in my junior year, considering what kind of research I should do, I was too impatient. I was eager to find a direction that had both research value and market potential, and also matched my past experiences. In the end, I spread myself too thin, unable to meet the demands of both technology and the market. Now looking back, that was quite unrealistic. I've talked to other master's and PhD students, and I've realized that people's directions also change, not to mention the new directions and demands that have arisen with the release of ChatGPT. "A one-size-fits-all" approach is fundamentally impossible; I should focus on gaining experiential knowledge from a technological standpoint, rather than blindly investing in a fixed direction. Personally, I think when you're young, you can experiment more and not rush to determine your path (though this is also a pitfall in its own right).

The second major pitfall is "lack of confidence". This was especially prominent during my junior and senior years, and has improved somewhat since coming to the United States. My lack of confidence has always come from my uncertain positioning and placing too much weight on many things, thinking too far ahead. For example, I used to think that doing a master's meant working hard on research or just getting a degree. But I've come to understand that some people can go to a better place for a platform's sake, which could also have been one of my choices. For me now, even though I don't particularly like my current project, I'm more inclined to accept it and make the right choices on top of it. From another perspective, I don't have much time to hesitate anymore. My previous lack of confidence came from always comparing my weaknesses to others' strengths, and wanting to compare myself to everyone, which put a lot of pressure on me. But now, I think I have strengths and weaknesses, and I'm not set in stone. So, focusing on studying and accumulating knowledge that suits me is the right choice. There's a saying, "accumulate steadily and reap abundantly," and I think that's very valuable.

The third major pitfall is "blindness and lack of information". To be honest, this description of me is a bit exaggerated, as I've always thought of myself as being good at socializing. It's just that looking back, I do have that feeling sometimes. For instance, questions about pursuing a Ph.D., entrepreneurship, or staying in the U.S., these are all things that could have been judged by discussing more with others. I think my abilities are relatively sufficient, but I lacked information during that time, which caused a lot of internal struggle. Therefore, if there's something I need to change first, it's that I hope to communicate more with others, get more information, ensure I don't miss opportunities, and also reduce my inner turmoil. From another perspective, I've always thought that "what to do" is important, and I still believe that. But at the same time, I've also realized that my skills are still lacking, so "how to do it" is also very much worth learning.

Overall, it's about slowing down the pace of life and increasing satisfaction.

Another thing worth mentioning is that over these three years, I've actually done a lot of exploration, even though sometimes I feel like I haven't put in my full effort, I've still taken things seriously and gained a lot. In 2021, I served as the head of the AI group and the head of the administrative department, entering a crazy 4-month period of overtime. I really felt busy and tired back then, but I learned about team management, administrative handling, and other things. In 2022, I participated in the autonomous driving and intelligent office chair projects, the first time I approached projects with an entrepreneurial mindset, realizing market thinking and design thinking. In 2023, I led a team of old classmates to build a paper analysis project called ZipPaper based on ChatGPT, and it was successfully launched. In this process, I learned about project progress management, user research, and more. Unfortunately, these three projects couldn't be sustained due to various reasons. I think it's very much related to my personal state, as each time it came to the time when I needed to advance my studies or find a job, I just couldn't continue.

The period from February to August each year is a period of ups and downs. To be honest, I'm not very satisfied. But I can accept it, after all, I don't currently have the courage and ability to fully engage in entrepreneurship. I hope I can persevere more, accumulate more.

About the future, it's flat, perhaps it's the years and experiences that have smoothed out my edges. I hope I can slow down first, become a mature member of society, and then start the next possible venture or endeavor. I believe life is about regression to the mean; I can always live the life I want.

Let's go!
